About Us

Pathways to Recovery Ontario is dedicated to supporting individuals and families affected by drug addiction. Our mission is to provide resources, education, and advocacy to help overcome addiction and rebuild lives.

Impacts of Drug Addiction

Drug addiction affects individuals, families, and communities in profound ways. It can lead to emotional distress, financial instability, and strained relationships. The impacts include:

  • On Individuals: Physical and mental health challenges, including increased risk of chronic diseases and mental health disorders.
  • On Relationships: Trust issues, conflict, and breakdowns in communication between couples and families.
  • On Families: Financial strain, caregiving burdens, and intergenerational trauma.

Resources and Support

We connect individuals and families with the tools they need for recovery, including:

  • Rehabilitation Centers: Providing safe spaces for detox and recovery.
  • Support Groups: Offering peer support and shared experiences.
  • Educational Programs: Raising awareness about the causes and solutions for addiction.
  • Emergency Hotlines: Immediate assistance for those in crisis (Call: 1-800-123-4567).

Theoretical Framework

We use the Bio-Psycho-Social Model to understand addiction. This model considers:

  • Biological Factors: Genetic predisposition and neurochemical imbalances.
  • Psychological Factors: Coping mechanisms, trauma, and mental health conditions.
  • Social Factors: Peer pressure, family dynamics, and societal norms.
